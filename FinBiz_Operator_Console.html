01 — Live

Call Flow

Top to bottom, fast. You run the call; let him talk ~60%. The urgency comes from his own answers in the Dig — not from anything you invent.

① Open — own it

Hi [Name], it's Ness at FinBiz Funding. Good time? I'll be straight and quick. I work with owners like you on growth capital — and I'm good at it. Give me five minutes. By the end, either I see a real way to put money to work in your business, or I don't — and I'll tell you flat. Fair? Normalizing a "no" disarms him and reads as confidence.

② Open the story

If money wasn't the bottleneck, what's the first move you'd make in the business? His picture here is what you close on later.

③ Gate — two numbers

So I'm not wasting your time — what's the business doing a month lately, and how long open? Clears the floor → ④ · Light → Light branch · Already funded → All Set branch.

④ Dig — make the gap real

What's the one thing holding you back from the next level right now? How long's that been going on? If it stays another six months — what's it cost you? Jobs you turn down, customers you lose? And if it were handled tomorrow — what changes for you? He just told you the pain AND the payoff, in his words. Don't pitch yet.

⑤ Point it + paint it

Here's what I'd put in front of you — a [term loan / line / advance]. Picture it: [the thing he said he'd do], funded and moving. That's the play. Depending on your numbers I'll get you the best terms I can — and I carry the paperwork. You run the business, I run this.

⑥ Close — hard, clean

Best time to grab capital is before you're desperate for it. Money's cheapest when you don't need it. Next step: send me four months of bank statements today, I build your offer tonight, we talk tomorrow with real numbers. No obligation — you see the full payback and decide. Let's get the options on the table.
↪ All set / already funded — push for the second swing Glad you're moving. Who'd you go with — payments daily, weekly, monthly? If those are daily pulls, I can probably refinance you into breathing room. Want me to take a real look? And the day you hit about halfway paid, that's a top-up at better terms. Let me be the call you make.
↪ Light — don't waste the call Straight with you — numbers are a little light today for the best products. But I'm not writing you off. Offer one: credit repair to get eligible (60–90 days; never promise a score) · CCP to hold the line · "Two stronger months and a callback."

03 — Structure

MCA Reality Check

An MCA advances capital upfront against future receivables, repaid daily or weekly. Walk a real calculation — the figures do the persuading.

Funded
$20,000
Factor
1.40
Total payback
$28,000
Term
100 pmts
Daily
$280
Non-negotiableA factor rate is not an APR. Never explain it as an interest rate — it's a fixed multiplier that sets the total payback. $20,000 × 1.40 = $28,000; ÷ 100 = $280/day.

The five loops every MCA conversation closes — skip one and you create a surprise at funding:

Funding amountExactly what lands in the account
Total paybackThe real obligation — not the funded figure
Payment frequencyDaily/weekly draws shape day-to-day cash flow
Cash-flow fitAn approval still has to be survivable to be a good deal
No obligationThe merchant reviews the offer before committing

04 — Triage

Eligibility & Lanes

You're not underwriting. Your call is narrower: is this file worth pursuing, and which risks have to be on the table before it ships? Needing money and being fundable are not the same thing.

Green

PUSH

  • 12+ months
  • 570+ credit
  • Consistent pattern, low NSFs
  • Manageable debt, fast cooperation

Yellow

REVIEW

  • 500–570 credit

08 — Defense

Objections, Deal Killers & Compliance

You're not overcoming resistance with pressure — you're reframing toward what the file can do. Acknowledge → clarify → reframe.

ObjectionYour reframe
"What's your rate?""I won't make a number up to keep you on the phone. The statements set the rate. Send them and I'll quote you real, today."
"I don't need funding.""Best time to look at options is before cash flow gets tight. If the numbers don't make sense, there's no obligation."
"I already have funding.""Who's the funder, what's the balance, how are payments structured? If the file supports it, we may improve your cash flow."
"I have bad credit.""That's fine — the revenue does the heavy lifting here, not the score. Let's pull the statements and see."
"It's too expensive.""Compared to what? If the money makes you more than it costs, the price answers itself. So what would you do with it?"
"Not interested.""Understood — would it help to know what's there before you actually need the capital?"

09 — Chase

Follow-Ups

Get the four months in, or get a callback. Never claim an offer before you've seen a file.

After a strong call

Recap + ask · SMS"Hey [Name], Ness from FinBiz — good talking. Send 4 months of business bank statements and I'll build your offer and come back with real numbers. No obligation."

Same-day nudge · SMS"[Name] — still got you on my list today. Fire over those 4 months and I'll get to work tonight."

Went quiet

Soft bump · SMS"[Name], didn't hear back — no rush. Just don't want to leave money on the table. Still want me to put options together?"

Door-open · SMS"All good if timing's off. When you're ready to look at capital I'm one text away. Check back end of week?"

Already funded

Back pocket · SMS"Glad you're handled. Keep my number — the day payments feel tight or you're ready for more, I can probably do better."

Halfway check-in · SMS"Figured you might be near halfway paid. That's usually when a top-up at better terms makes sense. Want me to look?"

Light / not yet

Honest hold · SMS"Numbers are a little light for the best products today — but I'm not writing you off. Couple stronger months and let's revisit."

Circle-back · SMS"Checking in like I said. How's revenue looking now? If it firmed up, let's pull statements and get you funded."

Offer on the table

Walk-through · SMS"Your numbers are ready. 5 minutes to walk the offer and total payback so you can decide? Today or tomorrow?"

Decision nudge · SMS"Offer's still good. No pressure — but the sooner we sign, the sooner it's in your account. Anything holding you up?"

Funded — renewal + referral

Renewal watch · SMS"Hope the funds did the job. Around halfway paid, you're eligible for fresh capital at better terms. Want me to flag you?"

Referral ask · SMS"Glad this worked out. Know another owner who could use capital? Send them my way — I'll take care of them."
