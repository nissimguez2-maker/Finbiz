01 — Live

Call Flow

Top to bottom, fast. Your eye lands on the next line, you say it, you shut up. The urgency comes from his answers in the Dig — not from anything you push.

One ruleLet him talk ~60%. You're steering, not selling — the file and his own words close this, not your volume.

① Open — own it

Hi [Name], it's Ness at FinBiz Funding. Good time? I'll be straight and quick. I work with owners like you on growth capital — and I'm good at it. Give me five minutes. By the end, either I see a real way to put money to work in your business, or I don't — and I'll tell you flat. Fair? Naming the "no" up front disarms him — reads as confidence, not a pitch.

② Open the story

If money wasn't the bottleneck, what's the first move you'd make in the business? Whatever he says here is what you paint back in ⑤. Write it down word for word.

③ Gate — two numbers

So I'm not wasting your time — what's the business doing a month lately, and how long you been open? Floor ~$15K+/mo · 6+ mo · 500+. Green $20K+ · 12+ mo · 570+. Clears the floor → ④ · Light → Light card · Already funded → All Set card.

④ Dig — make the gap real

Best time to grab capital is before you're desperate for it. You've got more leverage when you don't need it — and you're in a good spot to move now. What's the one thing holding you back from the next level right now? How long's that been going on? If it stays like that another six months — what's it cost you? Jobs you turn down, customers you lose? And if it were handled tomorrow — what changes for you? He just handed you the pain AND the payoff, in his words. Don't pitch yet — let it sit.

⑤ Point it + paint it

Good. Here's what I'd put in front of you — a [term loan / line / advance]. Picture it: [the thing he said he'd do], funded and moving. That's the play. Depending on your numbers I'll get you the best terms I can — and I carry the paperwork. You run the business, I run this. "Depending on your numbers" — never name a rate before you've seen the file.

⑥ Close — hard, clean

Here's what happens next: send me four months of bank statements today, I build your offer tonight, we talk tomorrow with real numbers. This your cell? Texting you right now so you've got me. Daisy-chain — cell → text → email. Don't hang up until you've got the email. → "Hey, it's Ness — FinBiz." → "Shoot me your email." No obligation — you see the full payback and you decide. But let's get the options on the table.
↪ All set / already funded — push for the second swing Glad you're moving. Who'd you go with — and are the payments daily, weekly, monthly? Here's where I'm worth your time: if those are daily pulls, I can probably refinance you into more breathing room. Want me to take a real look? And the day you hit about halfway paid, that's a top-up at better terms. I'll be the call you make — costs you nothing to have me in your corner. If he cracks the door, you're back at ⑥. Get the statements.
↪ Light — don't waste the call Straight with you — the numbers are a little light today for the best products. But I'm not writing you off. Offer one, then book the callback: credit repair to get eligible (60–90 days; never promise a score) · CCP to hold the line · "Give me two stronger months and a callback."

The ones that need a spoken line — say it like this:

MCA — the workhorse Primary It's an advance against your future receivables. I give you the capital now, you pay it back from sales over time. Factor rate + total payback only. Never call it interest, APR, or a bank loan. Non-bank — you don't owe me tax returns.
Term Loan vs MCA — the one place APR is fine This one's a real loan — fixed APR, monthly payments, from around 4.99%, no penalty if you pay it early. Only use APR language on the Term Loan. On an MCA it's a factor rate, never a rate. Rate here still depends on underwriting — no number before the file.
HELOC — speed is the hook It's a line against your property's equity. I can get you an indicative number — amount and rate — in about five minutes, and if you cooperate we can fund the same day. Needs 660+ credit AND real-estate equity. The 5-min quote is indicative; final terms confirm against the equity and title. Secured against his real estate.
Equipment Financing — collateral's built in We finance it against the equipment itself — up to the full cost, terms out to seven years. There may be write-offs too. Send him to his CPA on the tax piece — don't promise the write-off yourself.
Renewal / Refi / Consolidation — the second swing Once you're about halfway paid down, I can get you better terms — fresh capital, and breathing room from those daily pulls. Eligible around 50% paid down. This is the back-pocket play for prior-funded clients and stacked files.
Asset-Based & Specialty — factoring / SBA / CRE If you've got receivables or an elite profile, there's factoring, plus SBA and CRE for the top files. SBA and CRE are bank-funded — those need tax returns. Confirm bank vs non-bank before you ever ask a merchant for returns.
Relationship plays — never an openerCCP and Credit Repair don't lead a call. Use them mid-conversation to hold a merchant who's already funded or not yet eligible. Credit Repair runs up to $6K (scales with score) and takes 60–90 days to take effect — and CROA applies, so never guarantee a specific score or outcome.
Your railsNo "guaranteed." No rate before the file. Pitch what the file can support, not what sounds best — the file wins the argument in underwriting, every time.

04 — Triage

Eligibility & Lanes

You're not underwriting — you're triaging. The only question right now: is this file worth pursuing, and what risk has to be on the table before it ships? Floor to even start the conversation: $15K+/mo deposits, 6+ months in business, 500+ credit. Below that, needing money isn't the same as being fundable.

Green

PUSH
  • $20K+ deposits/mo
  • 12+ months
  • 570+ credit
  • Consistent pattern, low NSFs
  • Manageable debt, fast cooperation

Yellow

REVIEW
  • $10–20K deposits/mo
  • 3–12 months
  • 500–570 credit
  • Some explainable NSFs
  • One advance, low avg balance

Red

WEAK
  • Under $10K/mo · neg. now
  • Under 3 months
  • Frequent NSFs
  • Stacked advances, defaults
  • Liens/judgments · refuses docs
The ruleDon't pitch what sounds best — pitch what the file can actually support.

08 — Defense

Objections, Deal Killers & Compliance

You don't beat resistance with pressure — you reframe toward what the file can do. Acknowledge → clarify → reframe, every time. Top rows are the ones you'll hear most; scan down for the rest.

ObjectionYour reframe
"What's your rate?""Smart question — and I won't make a number up to keep you on the phone. The statements set the rate. Send them and I'll quote you real, today."
"Not interested.""Fair. Real quick — is it timing, or you just don't see the need right now?" (then reframe whichever he says)
"Too expensive.""Compared to what? If the money makes you more than it costs, the price answers itself. So what would you do with it if you had it?"
"Bad credit.""That's fine, honestly. The revenue does the heavy lifting here, not the score. Let's pull the statements and see what's there."
"I already have funding.""Who's the funder, what's the balance, how are the payments structured? If the file supports it, we may free up your cash flow."
"I don't need funding.""Best time to look at options is before cash flow gets tight. If the numbers don't make sense, there's no obligation."
